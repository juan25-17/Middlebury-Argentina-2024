Before visiting a hospital or private physician, students should contact Geo Blue to report their medical condition and request authorization. Geo Blue's telephone number is 001-610-254-8771, option 1. Each student's member number is on the Geoblue ID card provided by Middlebury by mail before arrival and, once in BA, on the emergency phone card provided at the beginning of the semester. However, if they do not have the card at that time, it is sufficient to mention their full name and the city where they are at the moment. In case of going to the hospital, bring a personal identification (original passport or photocopy). If, for any reason, a student visits a hospital without prior authorization from Geo Blue, or if he/she visits a doctor who is not affiliated with Geo Blue, and pays for the medical services received, he/she may request a reimbursement of expenses. To do so, the student will need to send Geo Blue (by mail) a Reimbursement Form and a copy of proof of the money spent. Geo Blue will also reimburse medication expenses, for which the Reimbursement Form and proof of expenses from the pharmacy must also be submitted.

SEXUAL ASSAULT VICTIMS’ ASSISTANCE IN THE CITY OF BUENOS AIRES

WITH CRIMINAL COMPLAINT

 A criminal complaint in case of sexual assault may be filed exclusively by the victim at the police station nearest the crime scene. It is important not to wash before reporting the crime.

 The police staff will summon the Flying Squad for Urgent Intervention with Sexual Assault Victims (Brigada Móvil de Intervención en Urgencias con Víctimas de Delitos Sexuales). The service is guaranteed 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. It operates only in the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires.

 A psychologist and a social worker are part of this squad, and they will assist the victim at the police station and later, take her to a hospital.

 There, preventive HIV treatment, antibiotics for sexually transmitted diseases (ETS, for its Spanish achronym) and emergency contraceptive medication will be provided, and a forensic  expert will examine the victim.

 Later, the victim will be taken to the Identification Unit (División de Individualización Criminal), where she will be able to identify the assailant through an identikit. She will be accompanied to the Forensic Medical Center as well, in accordance with the provisions of the Judge.

 Once the medical examination is over, the Flying Squad can also take the victim and her relatives home.

 This Team will also be present when supplemental reports or pictures recognition are needed in order to identify the assailant.

WITH CRIMINAL COMPLAINT

 In Argentina there is a National Protocol in place to assist victims of rape and sexual assault. This protocol states that the victim’s right to access medical attention and professional support prevails even when the (adult) victim decides not to report the crime.

 In case the victim prefers not to report the incident to the police, she must ask for preventive medication for HIV and sexually transmitted diseases at the Hospital Alemán Emergency  Room (at Av. Pueyrredón and Beruti) and, at her own discretion, for contraceptive medication.

In both cases, it is necessary to contact the Middlebury Program at once.
